Vaccination Schedule

Birth

Hepatitis B (HepB): Protects against the hepatitis B virus, which can cause liver disease. The first dose is given at birth.

1-2 Months

Hepatitis B (HepB): The second dose is given at 1-2 months of age.

2 Months

DTaP (Diphtheria, Tetanus, Pertussis): Protects against three serious diseases. The first dose is given at 2 months.

IPV (Inactivated Poliovirus): Protects against polio, which can cause paralysis. The first dose is given at 2 months.

Hib (Haemophilus influenzae type b): Protects against bacterial infections like meningitis. The first dose is given at 2 months.

PCV13 (Pneumococcal Conjugate): Protects against pneumococcal infections. The first dose is given at 2 months.

RV (Rotavirus): Protects against rotavirus, which causes severe diarrhea. The first dose is given at 2 months.

4 Months

DTaP, IPV, Hib, PCV13, RV: The second doses are given at 4 months.

6 Months

DTaP, IPV, Hib, PCV13, RV: The third doses are given at 6 months.

Hepatitis B (HepB): The third dose is given at 6-18 months.

Influenza: Annual vaccination starts at 6 months.

12-15 Months

MMR (Measles, Mumps, Rubella): Protects against three serious diseases. The first dose is given at 12-15 months.

Varicella (Chickenpox): Protects against chickenpox. The first dose is given at 12-15 months.

Hib, PCV13: Final doses are given at 12-15 months.

Hepatitis A (HepA): The first dose is given at 12-23 months.

15-18 Months

DTaP: The fourth dose is given at 15-18 months.

4-6 Years

DTaP, IPV, MMR, Varicella: Booster doses are given at 4-6 years.

11-12 Years

HPV (Human Papillomavirus): Protects against cancers caused by HPV. The vaccine series is started at 11-12 years.

MCV4 (Meningococcal): Protects against meningococcal disease. The first dose is given at 11-12 years.

Tdap (Tetanus, Diphtheria, Pertussis): Booster dose given at 11-12 years.

16 Years

MCV4 (Meningococcal): Booster dose given at 16 years.
